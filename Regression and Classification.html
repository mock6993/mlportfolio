None of my lasso models agree on the number of features. The two best, which perform equally to the other best models, use 9 or 10 features, which makes me think that one of the features in the 10 doesn't help the model perform better or worse. If chosing between the two, I would pick the one with 9 features since it is simpler and performs just as well

1.6 Discuss which final model you would choose to predict new data¶

For the sake of simplicity, I'd choose the OLS model. OLS, Ridge and Lasso models all performed the same except for the first lasso with alpha 1, which performed just slighly worse. When the scalers and gridsearch were applied, th model did slightly worse, so I wouldn't use any of those techniques.

The cross validation techniques tell me there is some variance in how the model performs at different cuts of the data, but the mean is somewhat close to the performance of the model (.78) and the performance doesn't vary widely from these models.
